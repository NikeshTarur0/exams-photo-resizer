Official Guidelines for Signature Uploads on Online Portals

Most online competitive exam sites (UPSC, SSC, IBPS, Railways) require candidates to upload a signature scanned on a white sheet of paper in blue/black ink. Scans with faint ink, ruled lines, or dark margins are automatically flagged by portal verification scripts.

Ink and Paper Guidelines

Signatures must be signed on pure white paper with a fresh black or blue ballpoint pen. Do not crop signatures written on lined notebook sheets or colored papers, as this fails automated background detection.

Image Enhancement Filter

Our built-in Enhance Signature Ink filter uses a smart threshold flood-fill filter to neutralize yellowish camera backgrounds to solid white, while increasing contrast on pen strokes to make signatures extremely legible.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the standard pixel size of an SSC signature?

For the Staff Selection Commission (SSC) portals, signatures must be rescaled to 140x60 pixels (minimum) to 280x140 pixels. The file size limit is strictly 10 KB to 20 KB.

Should the signature be in JPG or PNG format?

Virtually all Indian exam portals (including UPSC, IBPS, and state recruitments) require signatures to be uploaded in JPG or JPEG formats. PNGs containing alpha transparency layers are often rejected.

How does the Enhance Filter work?

It processes each pixel on a local HTML5 canvas. It isolates yellow shades typical of room-light phone cameras and converts them to pure white, while intensifying black and blue shades to enhance the stroke details.
