Converting PNG to JPG/JPEG Format for Online Forms

PNG format supports alpha transparency channels, making it great for icons but problematic for online application software. Portals like SSC, UPSC, and banking systems do not support PNG transparency profiles and often throw vague errors upon upload. Converting transparent PNGs to flat, solid-white JPGs solves these problems.

Automatic White Background fill

When converting transparent PNG signatures or logos to JPG, our script automatically overlays a solid white backdrop under the transparency layer to keep signatures legible.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the exam portal say PNG format is not supported?

PNG files are larger in size due to lossless compression and transparency data. Exam servers enforce strict JPG/JPEG formatting rules to keep database storage footprint light.

How is transparent color handled during conversion to JPEG?

JPEG format does not support transparency. Our converter overlays the transparent pixels with a solid white background color, making signatures look clean and official.

Is this converter completely safe for official documents?

Absolutely. Since the file is processed inside your local browser memory space, your personal documents never travel to remote web servers.
